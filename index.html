PhD in Machine learning in Music Pedagogy

Oct 2018 - Ongoing

University of Portsmouth, United Kingdom

The research question tries to uncover whether implementing Machine Learning Models into a pedagogical environment for a lecturer-centric subject, such as Music Theory, could positively influence the adoption rate of the teaching materials.

As part of the Research, a teaching and testing environment has been developed using Python that uses camera recognition components (blob detection and eye tracking) in combination with algorithmic timing to analyse learning patterns and restructure the teaching and testing materials as to maximise learning.

MSc in Music Technology

Sep 2017 - Sep 2018

Graduated with Merit

University of Portsmouth, United Kingdom

The Master of Science qualification represented the organic continuity for the BSc in Music and Sound Technology and featured a more detailed research of a niche topic. The research question for the Master asked whether integrating a small form-factor computer into a Eurorack Modular Synthesiser could be a viable product in an oversaturated market. This research question rose from and analysis of the Eurorack market which displayed an avalanche of digital modules that were all limited in terms of multitrack audio recording.

A Eurorack module has also been constructed and presented as part of the Final Submission which featured a LattePanda computer running an application developed using Juce and C++ for multi-track recording and Audio Effects stacking.
